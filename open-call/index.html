Processing Community Day // 2019 New York City

Open Call


Event Date: Saturday February 9, 10am - 5pm

Deadline for submissions: Friday December 14, 11:59pm

✨ Submission Form ✨

We welcome artists, programmers, activists, educators, hobbyists from all cultural, educational, and professional backgrounds to submit a proposal for a talk, workshop or facilitated conversation. If you have an idea that doesn’t fit our format, pick the closest one and let us know your idea. We are interested in proposals related to one of the following areas: ✨community building✨, ✨radical pedagogy, ✨art + code✨, ✨toolbuilding✨, ✨crafting with code✨, ✨game design✨, ✨sound art and music✨, ✨data visualization✨, ✨open source software✨.


The Department of Education is providing professional learning opportunity funding for elementary, middle and high school teachers in CS education to participate in Processing Community Day NYC to present talks, panels and workshops. For additional information, please contact apatel4@schools.nyc.gov.


Talk

Talk about your work, a concept, research, a project or other endeavor related to the themes of the Processing community. Your talk should be 30 minutes to an hour, including a Q & A with the audience.

Workshop

We are looking for a variety of workshops, including non-coding and hands-on sessions. Workshops should be 45 minutes to an hour. Workshops can be open to a broad community, beginner focused, or on an advanced topic.

Panel or Facilitated Conversation

This can take on multiple forms. You can lead a conversation with the entire audience on a particular topic. You can invite other guests and lead a formal panel. Or perhaps you have another idea that you’d like to propose.


Support

Processing Community Day NYC is an independent all volunteer-organized event in cooperation with the Processing Foundation and hundreds of other locally-organized nodes around the world. The New School is providing hosting support, with additional funding from Code + Liberal Arts at Eugene Lang. The event is supported by Department of Education Computer Science for All (CS4All), which allows us to make the event free for attendees and to provide honorariums and meals for speakers, workshop leaders and conversation facilitators. Speakers and workshop leaders will receive honorariums of $100. Panelists will receive honorariums of $75. Additional materials and equipment will be provided for workshops and talks when possible.
